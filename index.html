CSCI Honors – Urban Walkability & Subway Analysis

Hunter College · Fall 2025 · Projects HC 3 – HC 7

Project Overview

This website showcases the analysis of neighborhoods along the NYC 6 train line. Across assignments HC 3 through HC 7, I explore relationships between walkability, commute times, housing costs, demographics, and neighborhood desirability using data visualization and real-world datasets.

HC 3 – Commuting & Housing Costs

For HC 3, I compared commute times and rental prices across three neighborhoods: the most walkable, least walkable, and terminal neighborhoods along the 6 train. The analysis highlighted how much New Yorkers pay in rent to shorten their commute.

HC 4 – Subway Logo

Using matplotlib and hex color codes, I created a custom subway service logo for the Lexington Avenue Line (6).

HC 6 – Additional Data

In HC 6, I expanded the dataset by adding demographic features such as median household income, median age, population size, and average rent for each neighborhood studied. This richer dataset forms the basis for the visual analysis in HC 7.

HC 7 – Analysis Plots

HC 7 required creating three visualizations to explore which features most strongly influence the Niche neighborhood rankings. I focused on the relationships between:

  • Walkability and Niche Rank
  • Median Income and Niche Rank
  • Neighborhood Average Rent
